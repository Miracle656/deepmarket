# Spot YES/NO Markets

Permissionless prediction markets on a real central limit order book.
Anyone can create a question; YES and NO outcome tokens trade against each
other on **DeepBook V3**, and prices reflect the implied probability.

## How it works

Every market has:

- A **question** — "Will X happen by Y?"
- A **resolution time** — when the market settles
- A **quote asset** — SUI on testnet (USDC on mainnet later)
- A **vault** — SUI deposited to mint outcome tokens, paid out 1:1 to winners
- A **YES pool** and a **NO pool** on DeepBook V3 — outcome-token vs. quote

## Trading flow

<Steps>

### Mint outcome tokens

Deposit `X` SUI into the market's vault and receive `X` YES + `X` NO. The
two together always equal one unit of SUI — that's the no-arbitrage invariant.

### Trade YES or NO

Use DeepBook V3 to swap one side for the other. If you think YES is
underpriced, sell your NO for more YES (or buy YES directly with SUI). Prices
in cents (`¢`) reflect implied probability — `73¢` means "the market thinks
this is 73% likely."

### Wait for resolution

At resolution time, the market resolver calls `resolve_market(outcome: bool)`.
The vault is now redeemable by the winning side only.

### Redeem the winner

If YES wins, every `1 YES` redeems for `1 SUI` (and `NO` is worth zero).
If NO wins, vice versa. The losing side is burned.

</Steps>

## Composability with DeepBook V3

Each market registers two DeepBook V3 pools — `YES/QUOTE` and `NO/QUOTE`.
That means:

- Order types from V3 are inherited (limit, market, GTC, etc.)
- Liquidity is on a real CLOB, not an AMM curve
- Anything that integrates DeepBook (margin, lending, bots) can compose
  outcome tokens automatically

## skip-pools mode

For test markets where DEEP token cost matters, the contract supports
**skip-pools** — markets are minted without registering V3 pools. Tokens
can still be minted/redeemed but not order-book traded. Production markets
create pools normally.

## Per-market chat

Every spot market has an **encrypted chat room** attached. The Chart / Chat
tab strip on the market detail page switches between price view and the
end-to-end-encrypted room for that market. See
[Encrypted Market Chat](/products/chat).

## Indexer

The Rust + Axum indexer (`indexer/`, port 3000) polls Sui for
`market_factory` events, deduplicates them, and serves a REST API the
frontend uses for the markets list, fills, and volume.

<Callout type="info">
  The indexer is only required for Spot markets. DeepBook Predict reads from
  Mysten's public Predict server.
</Callout>
