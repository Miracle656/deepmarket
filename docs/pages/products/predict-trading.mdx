# Trading on Predict — step by step

A walkthrough for **traders** on the DeepBook Predict product. For the
protocol-level reference (Move calls, vault internals, SVI math), see
[DeepBook Predict](/products/deepbook-predict).

<Callout type="info">
  All actions described here happen on **Sui testnet**. Quote asset is **dUSDC**
  (not the official testnet USDC).
</Callout>

## Before you start

1. **A Sui wallet** — Slush extension, Slush web, Sui Wallet, or any
   wallet-standard wallet. Connect via the **Connect** button in the top right
   (or in the hamburger drawer on mobile).
2. **dUSDC** — Predict's quote asset. Request from the
   [dUSDC faucet form](https://tally.so/r/Xx102L). Allow a few minutes.
3. **A little SUI** — for gas. Use any Sui testnet faucet.

## Trade a binary option (UP / DOWN)

<Steps>

### Open the oracle list

Go to **/predict**. You'll see active oracles (rolling sub-hour BTC expiries)
sorted by **Expiring soon** by default. Switch sort to **Newest** or
**Furthest** with the toggle in the top right of the list.

Each oracle card shows the underlying, status (Active / Pending / Settled),
expiry (`in Xm` / `in Xh`), and the min-strike floor.

### Pick an oracle

Click an **Active** oracle. You land on the oracle detail page with four chart
tabs at the top:

- **Price** — BTC spot line from Mysten's predict-server.
- **Vol Smile** — live SVI smile (log-moneyness × implied vol) from the
  on-chain params the vault prices off.
- **Candles** — real BTC OHLC candlesticks from GMX's public keeper.
- **Trades** — live mint/redeem feed on this oracle from `/trades/:id`.

The mint sidebar is on the right.

### First time only — initialize a PredictManager

The sidebar shows **Init manager** before your first mint. Click it and
sign — this creates your dUSDC margin account (a shared object). The id is
cached in localStorage; subsequent visits skip this step.

After this, you can have multiple managers per address (e.g. one for the web
app, one for the Telegram bot).

### Pick your view: Binary or Range

Toggle **Binary | Range** at the top of the mint sidebar.

- **Binary** — bet BTC settles **above** (UP) or **at-or-below** (DOWN) a
  single strike. Pays `quantity` dUSDC if right, $0 if wrong.
- **Range** — bet BTC settles **within a band** `[lower, higher]`. Pays
  `quantity` if right, $0 if wrong.

### Pick a strike (Binary)

You'll see an **11-strike grid centered on rounded spot**. Strikes step by the
oracle's `tick_size`. Click a strike; pick **UP** or **DOWN**.

For Range mode: two grids (Lower / Higher), with overlap states disabled.

### Pick a quantity

Quick chips: `$1`, `$5`, `$10`, `$25`. Or type a custom max payout. This is
**how much you'll receive if you're right** — your cost is always less than
quantity (it's the implied probability × quantity).

The sidebar live-quotes:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | What you pay in dUSDC now |
| **Payout** | What you get if right (= `quantity`) |
| **Implied prob** | `cost / quantity` as a % |

These come from a **350ms-debounced devInspect of `predict::get_trade_amounts`** —
no tx, no gas; updates live as you change strike/qty.

### Confirm & sign

Click **Mint UP @ $X** (or **DOWN**). One signature. The tx:

1. Deposits dUSDC from your wallet into the manager (if needed)
2. Constructs the `MarketKey` for `(oracle, expiry, strike, is_up)`
3. Calls `predict::mint` for `quantity`

The position appears under **Your positions** in the sidebar.

### Sell early (optional)

Before expiry, click **Sell $X** on the position row to redeem at the live
**bid**. The payout (mark price × quantity) returns to your manager. Use this
to take profit (or cut loss) without waiting for settlement.

### Wait for settlement

When the oracle expires (`status: pending` → `settled`):

- In-the-money positions transition to **won** / **redeemable**.
- Out-of-the-money positions transition to **lost**.

Click the per-position button:

- ⭐ **Claim $X** for winners — payout flows to the manager.
- 🗑 **Clear position** for losers — closes the row.

### Withdraw to wallet

When you want to take dUSDC out of the manager: top of the sidebar shows
**Withdraw $X → wallet**. One tx; dUSDC returns to your address.

</Steps>

## Be the house — supply liquidity to the vault

DeepBook Predict's vault is the **counterparty to every option**. LPs supply
dUSDC into the vault and earn the premiums takers pay. Vault yield comes from:

- **Edge in pricing** — the SVI surface includes a spread, so on average the
  vault sells options slightly above fair value.
- **Time decay** — premium decays as expiry approaches.

<Steps>

### Open the Vault page

Go to **/vault**. You'll see vault stats:

- **TVL** — total dUSDC held by the vault.
- **Available** — withdrawable headroom (= balance − max payout).
- **Utilization** — exposed-payout / TVL.

Plus **Your position**: your **PLP** shares + current value at the live share
price.

### Supply dUSDC

In the **Supply** tab, enter an amount (or hit **Max** = your wallet dUSDC),
then **Supply liquidity**. One signature. You receive **PLP** tokens
representing your share of the vault. PLP shares accrue value as the vault
profits.

<Callout type="info">
  Supply/withdraw txs assert the vault's mark-to-market is fresh — DeepMarket
  automatically calls `refresh_oracle_mtm` for live exposed oracles in the same
  PTB so the tx never aborts on stale MTM.
</Callout>

### Watch the share price

Your **PLP balance** is constant; the **share price** (NAV per PLP) grows over
time as the vault earns. **Your position value** = balance × share price.

### Withdraw

In the **Withdraw** tab, enter a dUSDC amount (or **Max** for your full
position). The frontend converts to the right PLP share count, burns them, and
returns dUSDC. One signature.

</Steps>

## Other surfaces

- **/portfolio** — every PredictManager you own, with a cumulative realized
  **P&L chart** (from `/managers/:id/pnl`), per-manager balances, and open
  positions.
- **/leaderboard** — every PredictManager ranked by realized P&L. Your own
  managers are highlighted.
- **/predict/:id → Trades tab** — live mint/redeem feed for that oracle.
- **Telegram bot** — `/strategy` lets an autonomous Claude-powered agent mint
  binaries on your behalf with an on-chain spend cap. See
  [the agent docs](/agent/llm-agent).

## Tips

- **Near-spot binaries are not free money.** A 50¢ binary needs ~50% win rate
  to break even — and short-dated near-spot bets have high gamma. Read the
  smile.
- **Premium tax.** The bid is below the fair mid; selling early always loses
  some edge to the vault's spread.
- **Settle before you withdraw.** Open positions lock against the manager's
  trading balance; redeeming/settling frees it up for withdrawal.
- **Range options beat directional bets in chop.** If you think the underlying
  will stay rangebound, a `[lower, higher]` mint pays more for the same risk
  budget than two opposing binaries.

## Common errors

| Message | What it means |
|---|---|
| `EAskPriceOutOfBounds` | Strike is outside the vault's quote band; pick a closer strike. |
| `Insufficient trading balance` | Deposit more dUSDC into the manager first. |
| `ENoDataPoints` | Vault MTM is stale; usually resolves in seconds — retry. |
| `redeem failed (not settled)` | Oracle is still live. Either wait or use **Sell** for the live bid. |
