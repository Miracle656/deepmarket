# Trading Guide

How to actually place a trade on a DeepBook-pool market, end to end. The
underlying engine is a real on-chain central limit order book (DeepBook
V3) — the UI hides the plumbing so you only deal with **Buy / Sell** and
a **price**.

<Callout type="info">
Look for the badge on each market card. **`ORDER BOOK`** markets are
tradeable (live DeepBook pools). **`MINT ONLY`** markets support just
minting and redeeming — there's no order book to trade against.
</Callout>

## What you need

- A Sui wallet (e.g. **Slush**) connected, with some **testnet SUI** for
  gas and the trade itself.
- A little **DEEP** — DeepBook charges trading fees in DEEP. The app
  auto-deposits a small amount from your wallet on each order, so you just
  need a few DEEP sitting in your wallet. Grab some from the DeepBook
  testnet faucet if you have none.

## Place your first trade

<Steps>

### Connect your wallet

Top-right → Connect. Open any **`ORDER BOOK`** market.

### Enable trading (one-time)

The first time you trade a given market, click **⚡ Enable trading**. This
single transaction:

- Creates your DeepBook **BalanceManager** (your on-chain trading account), and
- Seeds the market's pools with a **DEEP price reference** so fees can be
  computed.

You only do this once per market. After it confirms, the Buy/Sell form
appears.

### Choose Limit or Market

- **Limit** — you post a resting order at *your* price. This is what
  *adds* liquidity. On a brand-new market the book is empty, so the first
  orders **must** be limit orders.
- **Market** — fills instantly against orders already on the book. Does
  nothing if the book is empty.

<Callout type="warning">
The active mode is the **solid-filled** button. If you try a Market order
on an empty book it simply fills nothing — switch to Limit and post a
price to seed the market.
</Callout>

### Pick a side and size

- **YES** wins if the event happens; **NO** wins if it doesn't.
- Price is in **SUI per share, 0–1** — and that *is* the implied
  probability. `0.62` = the market pricing it at 62%. YES price + NO price
  always sums to 1.
- For a **Limit** order, enter a **price** and a **quantity (shares)**.
  For a **Market** buy, enter an **amount of SUI**.

### Submit

Click **Buy / Sell** (market) or **Post Bid / Ask** (limit) and approve in
your wallet. The app handles fee deposit and order-size rounding for you.

</Steps>

## Selling a side you don't own

To *sell* YES (or NO) you must hold those tokens first. Two ways to get
them:

1. **Buy** them on the order book (a market or limit buy), or
2. **Mint** a pair: click **Mint Tokens**, deposit `X` SUI, and receive
   `X` YES **and** `X` NO. Then sell whichever side you don't want — that
   nets you a position on the side you keep, funded by the sale.

Minting is also how a market creator **seeds liquidity**: mint a pair,
then post a YES ask *and* a NO ask so others have something to trade
against.

## Resolution & redeeming

At the resolution time, the market is resolved to YES or NO. Open the
market and click **Redeem** — the winning token pays out 1:1 in SUI from
the vault; the losing token is worth nothing.

## Advanced

Under **Advanced** in the trade panel:

- **Re-sync DEEP price** — only needed if a pool can't price fees (rare,
  after long inactivity).
- **Cancel my open orders** — cancels all your resting orders on the
  selected outcome's pool and returns the locked collateral to your
  BalanceManager.

<Callout type="info">
Funds you deposit to back a resting order (or that arrive from a fill)
live in your DeepBook **BalanceManager** until you withdraw them. A
withdraw UI is on the roadmap; for now, cancelling orders returns their
collateral to the manager.
</Callout>

## Why an order book (vs. Polymarket)

Most prediction markets run a **centralized, off-chain** matching engine —
an operator you trust not to censor, freeze, or front-run. DeepMarket
matches **fully on-chain via DeepBook V3**: every order and fill is public
and verifiable, with no privileged operator. The tradeoff is that new
markets need someone to post the first orders (no automatic market maker
yet) — see the [roadmap](/reference/roadmap).
