🎯 How DeepMarket Trading Works

Explained like you're five. No finance words without a plain meaning next to them.

The one big idea: A prediction market is a place where people bet on what will happen. "Will Arsenal win?" "Who wins the World Cup?" You buy a little ticket for the answer you believe in. If you're right, your ticket turns into money. If you're wrong, it's worth nothing.

1. Tickets (tokens) 🎟️

When you bet on an answer, you get a token. A token is just a ticket that says "I picked this answer." It lives in your wallet (Slush).

  • If your answer wins → your ticket is worth money. You trade it in for coins.
  • If your answer loses → your ticket is worth nothing. 😢
Every market makes its own tickets. Market #4's "YES" ticket and market #7's "YES" ticket are different things — even if they're both called YES. We now print the market's name on the ticket (like YES · Will Arsenal win…) so your wallet shows which market it belongs to.

2. Two kinds of markets

YES / NO marketMany-Choice market (multi-outcome)
Only two answers. YES or NO.
"Will Arsenal win? Yes or No."		 Many answers, one ticket each.
"Who wins the World Cup? Spain / France / England / Argentina."
Tickets named YES / NO. Tickets named after each choice (SPAIN, FRANCE…). That's why you can tell which market it's from. 👍

3. Two ways to get a ticket

Way A — Stake / Mint (buy a brand-new ticket from the market) 🏭

You give the market some SUI (the money), and it prints you a fresh ticket.

  • Many-Choice market: the button is "Stake". You stake SUI on Spain → you get Spain tickets. (That is the minting — there's no separate "Mint" button because you only ever get the one choice you picked.)
  • YES/NO market: the button is "Mint Tokens". You put in SUI and get both a YES and a NO ticket (a matched pair). You keep the side you believe and can sell the other.
Why the difference? In Many-Choice you back one horse, so you get one ticket. In YES/NO the two sides always add up to one whole, so minting gives you the whole set (one YES + one NO).

Way B — The Marketplace (buy a ticket from another person) 🤝

Instead of printing a new ticket, you can buy someone else's ticket, or sell yours. This happens on the order book (a list of offers). More on that below.

4. Price = Chance 🎲

A winning ticket is always worth exactly 1 SUI. A losing ticket is worth 0. So the price people pay for a ticket tells you how likely they think it is:

  • 50¢ means the crowd thinks it's a 50% chance.
  • 17¢ means about a 17% chance.
A ticket can never cost more than 100¢ (1 SUI) — nobody pays more than $1 for something that pays back at most $1. So the chance never goes above 100%. (Our app blocks any price of 1 or more.)

5. The order book 📓

The order book is a table of offers waiting to happen. Two kinds of offers:

WordPlain meaning
Bid "I want to buy a ticket for this price." (a buyer waiting)
Ask "I want to sell my ticket for this price." (a seller waiting)
Resting orderAn offer just sitting on the table, waiting for someone to take it.
Fill an orderTo take someone's waiting offer — the trade happens. ✅
Limit vs Market — the two ways to make an offer:
• Limit = "Only at my price." It may wait on the table (this is how you add an offer / seed the book).
• Market = "Right now, best price available." It takes a waiting offer immediately (this is how you fill).

When a Bid and an Ask meet at the same price, they shake hands and disappear — that's a completed trade. After they're gone, the book can look empty again. That's normal.

6. The other scary words 🧸

WordWhat it really is
DEEPA tiny coin used to pay the marketplace's small fee. Like a token for the arcade machine.
BalanceManagerYour personal locker at the marketplace. Money/tickets for your offers sit in here.
Enable tradingOpen your locker for the first time + tell this market how to charge the tiny fee. One-time.
Sync DEEP priceWake up the fee-meter for one choice. Do it once before your first offer on that choice.
Claim filled balancesTake your winnings out of the locker and into your wallet, after an offer was taken.
ResolveThe referee says which answer won. Only the admin can do this.
RedeemTrade your winning ticket in for real coins.
Why is there "Claim" in Many-Choice but not in YES/NO?
In YES/NO, the quick "Buy/Sell" buttons use Market orders, which automatically empty your locker back to your wallet — so there's nothing left to claim. In Many-Choice you place Limit offers that wait, so after one is taken the winnings sit in your locker until you press Claim. (Heads up: if you place a limit offer in a YES/NO market too, it also leaves money in the locker — that's a small thing we can add a Claim button for there later.)

7. Do a real trade with two friends 👫

A trade needs two people: one selling, one buying. We use two accounts in your wallet (switch accounts in the Slush extension; the app follows whoever is selected).

Example: trade "Spain" on the World Cup market

1
Account A makes an offer (Limit). Pick Spain → Limit tab → if it's your first time, press Sync DEEP price once → type a price (e.g. 0.40, meaning 40¢) and a quantity (e.g. 5 tickets) → press Bid (buy) or Ask (sell). (To sell, you must own Spain tickets first — Stake some SUI on Spain to get them.)
2
That offer now waits on the order book.
3
Account B takes it (the opposite side). Switch to account B → pick Spain → if A made a Bid at 40¢, B places an Ask at 40¢ (or lower); if A made an Ask, B places a Bid. They meet → trade happens. ✅
4
Both press "Claim filled balances" to move the results into their wallets.
About "quantity": price is in SUI per ticket (0–1). Quantity is how many tickets. So "price 0.40, quantity 5" = an offer about 5 tickets at 40¢ each = 2 SUI total.
"Am I staking from account 1?" You can use either account — they're just two people. You only need to stake if you want to sell (you must own tickets to sell them). To buy (a Bid), you just need SUI, no staking. For a quick demo: Account A stakes a little and posts an Ask; Account B posts a Bid that matches it.

8. When the market ends 🏁

1
Cancel waiting offers first. Any offer still on the book gets stuck (its money stays locked) and can be picked off unfairly once the winner is known. Cancel them to get your money back.
2
Resolve. The admin presses Win on the correct answer (or YES/NO). The referee has spoken.
3
Redeem. Everyone holding the winning ticket trades it in for SUI. Losing tickets are worth nothing.
The whole journey in one line: Pick an answer → get a ticket (Stake/Mint or Buy) → maybe trade it on the order book → wait for the result → if you won, Redeem for money. 🎉
